You Can't Put Your Arms Around a Memory Single by Z Berg and Ryan Ross

Description

In the fall of 2016, Ryan purchased an instrument called the French Connection (actually a CV controller for those who are curious) which was a recreation of the historic Ondes Martenot. The Ondes Martenot is one of the earliest synthesizers, developed by Maurice Martenot in the 1930s after his service as a radio engineer in World War I. I highly recommend watching Wavemakers (original French title Le chant des ondes, 2012 directed by Caroline Martel) for those unfamiliar with the instrument.

After first playing on the French Connection, I remember driving home and thinking that I could never go back to playing the piano as this new instrument is so much more expressive. Luckily within the next two years, I met Nao Omo, the inventor of the Ondomo, a fully-functioning recreation of the Ones Martenot and I was able to get one of the early models. To this day, it is still among my most prized possessions.

In 2019, Ryan along with Z Berg, Alex Greenwald and I performed a version of You Can't Put Your Arms Around a Memory live (video here: ), which sparked Tony Berg to encourage us to create a recording. The final recording features an arrangement for 3 Ondomo.
